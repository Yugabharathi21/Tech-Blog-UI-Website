How to Boost HDD Performance in Windows 10/11 Easily


Are you troubled by slow HDD speed and want to know how to boost HDD performance
on Windows 10? On this page, you will get 6 easy methods.
By Jonna • Updated on January 23, 2024

Is There a Way to Improve HDD Performance?
HDDs (hard disk drives) are one of the most commonly used types of computer storage.
People use them as a medium for storing files, media, documents, and other types of data.
However, as you use them for a long period, you may find that your hard disk lags when running
programs, starting games, or accessing files.
Why would this happen? How to boost HDD performance on Windows 10 or 11? In this guide,
we will show you the common reasons for HDD latency and effective techniques to speed up
your HDD performance.

Why is HDD Running Slow on Windows 10/11?

It's a fact that the performance of the hard drives will degrade with the wear
and tear from lots of reading and writing. This can lead to PC slow startup, programs running slow,
or occasional system crashes. Some common reasons for this slowdown include:

  • Fragmentation of data on HDD leads to slow read/write speeds.
  • There are bad sectors on the hard drive.
  • Lots of temporary files and other data take up disk space.
  • Viruses or malware damage your hard drive, causing your computer to behave erratically.

How to Boost HDD Performance in Windows 10/11 in 6 Methods

In the following, six solutions about how to speed up HDD on Windows 11/10 with be introduced with full guides.


  1. Defrag HDD on Windows 10/11

      2. Defragmenting your hard drive is a fundamental step in boosting its performance.
      When you use your hard drive for a long time, files on your disk become fragmented and
      scattered in various locations. This can slow down its read and write operations.
      Defragmentation rearranges these files,
      making them organized into contiguous blocks for faster access. To do so, follow these steps:

    • Press Win + S to open the search bar. Type "defrag" and open the Defragment and Optimize Drives app.

    • Select the disk drive you want to optimize.
    • Click on the Optimize button to defragment and optimize your hard drive.
  2. Remove Temporary Files

    3. Deleting temporary files is a simple yet effective way about how to boost HDD performance
    in Windows 10. This is because browsers apps will download lots of temporary files,
    which can occupy the hard drive space and also slow it down. To remove temporary files:

    • Open the Run menu by pressing "Windows + R", type "temp” and press Enter.

    • Press Ctrl + A to select all the temporary files in the Temp folder and press Shift + Delete to delete them.

      • You can also run the Windows Disk Cleanup tool to free up space on Windows by typing cleanmgr
      in Windows search box, opening the tool,
      choosing the drive you want to clean up, and select files to delete.
